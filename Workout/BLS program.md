### BLS Workout Program

**Program Structure:**

- **Frequency:** 4 days per week
- **Split:** Upper/Lower body
- **Focus:** Compound lifts with some accessory exercises
- **Progression:** Progressive overload (increase weight gradually)

**Weekly Schedule:**

- **Day 1:** Upper Body (Chest, Shoulders, Triceps)
- **Day 2:** Lower Body (Legs, Abs)
- **Day 3:** Rest
- **Day 4:** Upper Body (Back, Biceps)
- **Day 5:** Lower Body (Legs, Abs)
- **Day 6:** Rest
- **Day 7:** Rest or Light Activity (Optional)

#### Day 1: Upper Body (Chest, Shoulders, Triceps)

1.  **Bench Press:** 3 sets of 4-6 reps
    - Focus on lifting heavy with proper form. This compound movement targets the chest, shoulders, and triceps.
2.  **Overhead Press:** 3 sets of 4-6 reps
    - A compound exercise for the shoulders and triceps. Ensure a full range of motion.
3.  **Incline Bench Press:** 3 sets of 4-6 reps
    - Targets the upper chest and shoulders. Use a moderate incline (about 30 degrees).
4.  **Dumbbell Flyes:** 3 sets of 8-10 reps
    - Isolation exercise for the chest. Focus on a slow, controlled movement.
5.  **Tricep Dips:** 3 sets of 6-8 reps
    - A compound movement for the triceps. Can be done on parallel bars or a bench.

#### Day 2: Lower Body (Legs, Abs)

1.  **Squat:** 3 sets of 4-6 reps
    - A key compound movement for the quads, hamstrings, glutes, and core.
2.  **Deadlift:** 3 sets of 4-6 reps
    - Targets the entire posterior chain, including the lower back, glutes, and hamstrings.
3.  **Leg Press:** 3 sets of 8-10 reps
    - Focuses on the quads and glutes. Ensure proper foot placement and range of motion.
4.  **Leg Curls:** 3 sets of 8-10 reps
    - Isolation exercise for the hamstrings.
5.  **Hanging Leg Raise:** 3 sets of 10-12 reps
    - Effective for the lower abs. Perform slowly for maximum tension.

#### Day 4: Upper Body (Back, Biceps)

1.  **Pull-Ups:** 3 sets of 4-6 reps (or Lat Pulldowns if pull-ups are too challenging)
    - Compound movement for the lats, biceps, and shoulders.
2.  **Barbell Row:** 3 sets of 4-6 reps
    - Targets the upper back, lats, and biceps. Keep the back straight and lift with control.
3.  **One-Arm Dumbbell Row:** 3 sets of 8-10 reps
    - Focuses on the lats and mid-back. Ensure a full range of motion.
4.  **Face Pulls:** 3 sets of 8-10 reps
    - Great for the rear delts and upper back. Use a cable machine for this exercise.
5.  **Barbell Curls:** 3 sets of 6-8 reps
    - Primary bicep exercise. Keep elbows close to the body and avoid swinging.

#### Day 5: Lower Body (Legs, Abs)

1.  **Deadlift (or Romanian Deadlift):** 3 sets of 4-6 reps
    - If you performed conventional deadlifts on Day 2, do Romanian deadlifts here to vary the stimulus on your hamstrings and glutes.
2.  **Front Squat:** 3 sets of 4-6 reps
    - Emphasizes the quads more than the back squat. Maintain an upright torso.
3.  **Lunges:** 3 sets of 8-10 reps per leg
    - Great for quads, hamstrings, and glutes. Perform walking or stationary lunges.
4.  **Calf Raises:** 3 sets of 12-15 reps
    - Focus on a full range of motion and a good stretch at the bottom.
5.  **Plank:** 3 sets of 1 minute
    - Core stabilization exercise. Keep the body straight and engage the core
