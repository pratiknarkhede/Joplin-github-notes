## Study Strategy

1.  **Mixed Approach**: This schedule mixes topics daily. This helps in maintaining interest and reinforces connections between different data structures and algorithms.
2.  **Time Management**:
    - Spend 45-60 minutes per question on weekdays.
    - Allocate 90-120 minutes for algorithm implementation on Sundays.
3.  **Question Approach**:
    - First 15 minutes: Try to solve the problem on your own.
    - Next 15 minutes: If stuck, look at hints or the solution approach (not the full code).
    - Last 15-30 minutes: Implement the solution and optimize it.
4.  **Solution Review**:
    - It's okay to look at solutions, but do so strategically.
    - After attempting the problem, review the optimal solution to learn new techniques.
    - Always try to understand why a particular approach is better.
5.  **Implementation**:
    - Always code the solution in Java, even if you looked at the approach.
    - Focus on writing clean, efficient code.
6.  **Review Process**:
    - At the end of each week, review all problems solved.
    - Create short notes or flashcards for key concepts and patterns.
7.  **Spaced Repetition**:
    - Revisit problems from previous weeks regularly.
    - Try to solve them again without looking at previous solutions.
8.  **Common Algorithms Focus**:
    - Dedicate Sundays to implementing and understanding common algorithms.
    - These form the foundation for many interview questions.
9.  **Mock Interviews**:
    - After 3-4 weeks, start doing mock interviews with friends or online platforms.
    - This helps in improving problem-solving speed and communication skills.
10. **Continuous Learning**:
    - Keep track of problem-solving patterns you encounter.
    - Regularly update your study plan based on your progress and areas that need more focus.
